## What is the difference between AI and AR try-on?

AI-generated try-on creates an output image from shopper and product inputs. Augmented-reality try-on places or tracks a digital item in a live camera view. Neither approach is automatically better for every product or shopper task.

## When is each approach useful?

| Question | AI-generated try-on | AR try-on |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary output | Generated image | Live or camera-based overlay |
| Useful for | Visualizing a styled result | Inspecting placement or scale in real time |
| Evaluation focus | Image quality, wait time, and consistency | Tracking, alignment, device support, and responsiveness |
| Common implementation concern | Generation reliability and clear expectations | Camera permissions and device/browser behavior |

## How should a merchant choose?

1. Define the customer question the experience must answer.
2. Test representative products and shopper devices.
3. Review the full flow, including failures and retries.
4. Measure engagement, add-to-cart behavior, conversion, support questions, and returns.
5. Compare total cost and operational effort using current vendor information.

<Warning>
  Vendor performance and pricing change. Do not choose a solution from unsourced benchmark percentages or an old comparison table.
</Warning>
