## Why Image Quality Matters

Virtual try-on accuracy depends heavily on **image quality**. Poor photos lead to inaccurate results, while high-quality images deliver realistic, helpful visualizations.

## Product Image Issues

### Issue: Try-on results look unrealistic

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Busy or Patterned Background">
    **Problem:** AI struggles to isolate garment from complex backgrounds

    **What it looks like:**
    - Background elements appear in try-on
    - Garment edges are fuzzy or incorrect
    - Color bleeding from background

    **Solution:**
    1. Re-shoot product on plain white or gray background
    2. Use background removal tools (Remove.bg, Photoshop)
    3. Ensure high contrast between garment and background
    4. See [image requirements guide](/product-setup/image-requirements)

    **Quick fix:** Use editing software to replace background with solid color.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Poor Lighting">
    **Problem:** Dark,shadowy, or unevenly lit product photos

    **What it looks like:**
    - Colors appear darker than actual product
    - Details lost in shadows
    - Inaccurate rendering

    **Solution:**
    1. Re-shoot with soft, even lighting (studio lights or natural light)
    2. Avoid harsh shadows
    3. Use photo editing to increase brightness and reduce shadows
    4. Aim for consistent lighting across all product images

    **Quick fix:** Use photo editor to adjust exposure +20-30% and increase shadows.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Low Resolution">
    **Problem:** Pixelated or blurry product images

    **What it looks like:**
    - Fuzzy try-on results
    - Loss of detail
    - Artifacts in rendering

    **Solution:**
    1. Use minimum 800px width images (1200px+ recommended)
    2. Don't upscale small images (will be blurry)
    3. Re-shoot products at higher resolution
    4. Export at high quality (90%+ JPEG quality)

    **Quick fix:** If you must use existing images, ensure they're at least 800px wide.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Garment Not Centered or Cropped">
    **Problem:** Product is off-center, angled, or partially cropped

    **What it looks like:**
    - Garment positioned incorrectly on body
    - Incomplete rendering (missing sleeves, neckline)
    - Distorted placement

    **Solution:**
    1. Center garment in frame (60-80% of image)
    2. Include full neckline, sleeves, and hem
    3. Shoot from straight-on angle (not above or below)
    4. Crop strategically to remove unnecessary space

    **Quick fix:** Re-crop image to center garment properly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Garment Blends with Background">
    **Problem:** Low contrast between product and background

    **What it looks like:**
    - AI can't determine garment edges
    - Parts of garment missing
    - Inaccurate silhouette

    **Solution:**
    1. Use white background for dark garments
    2. Use gray background for light garments
    3. Increase contrast in photo editing
    4. Add slight border/shadow to separate garment

    **Quick fix:** Adjust contrast +20-30% in photo editor.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Selfie Quality Issues

### Issue: Uploaded selfie causes poor results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Dark or Poorly Lit Selfie">
    **Problem:** Selfie taken in low light

    **What it looks like:**
    - "No face detected" error
    - Inaccurate rendering
    - Poor skin tone representation

    **Solution for merchants:**
    1. Add instructions near try-on button: "Use good lighting for best results"
    2. Show example good selfie vs. bad selfie
    3. Provide tips on product page

    **Solution for customers:**
    1. Take selfie in natural light or bright indoor lighting
    2. Face a window or light source
    3. Avoid backlighting (light behind you)
    4. Use phone's flash if needed
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Angled or Profile Selfie">
    **Problem:** Face not facing camera directly

    **What it looks like:**
    - "No face detected" error
    - Garment positioned incorrectly
    - Distorted rendering

    **Solution:**
    1. Add instructions: "Face the camera directly"
    2. Show visual example (front-facing selfie icon)
    3. Reject angled photos in upload flow (if possible)

    **Customer guidance:** Face camera straight-on, not at an angle.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sunglasses or Hat Obstructing Face">
    **Problem:** Face partially obscured

    **What it looks like:**
    - "No face detected" error
    - Inaccurate face mapping

    **Solution:**
    1. Add instructions: "Remove sunglasses and hats"
    2. Show clear face example

    **Customer guidance:** Remove accessories that cover face.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Cropped or Zoomed Selfie">
    **Problem:** Face is too zoomed in or partially cropped

    **What it looks like:**
    - Face detection fails
    - Body positioning is off

    **Solution:**
    1. Add instructions: "Include your head and shoulders"
    2. Show proper framing example

    **Customer guidance:** Don't zoom too close – show head and upper body.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Blurry or Low-Resolution Selfie">
    **Problem:** Selfie is out of focus or very low quality

    **What it looks like:**
    - Fuzzy or inaccurate rendering
    - Loss of detail

    **Solution:**
    1. Add minimum resolution requirement (recommend 800px+)
    2. Reject very low-res photos

    **Customer guidance:** Use phone's main camera, not front camera if quality is poor.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Color Accuracy Issues

### Issue: Colors don't match expectations

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Product Image Color is Inaccurate">
    **Problem:** Source product photo has wrong colors

    **Solution:**
    1. Color-correct product images in photo editor
    2. Use color-calibrated monitor for editing
    3. Compare to physical product
    4. Adjust white balance, saturation, hue

    **Note:** Virtual try-on can only be as accurate as your product photos.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Selfie Lighting Affects Color Perception">
    **Problem:** Colored lighting in selfie (yellow, blue tint)

    **Solution:**
    1. Instruct customers to use neutral lighting
    2. Avoid selfies taken in direct sunlight (yellow)
    3. Avoid indoor tungsten lighting (yellow)
    4. Recommend natural daylight or neutral LED

    **Cannot be fixed:** If selfie has colored lighting, result will reflect that.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Screen Color Differences">
    **Problem:** Different devices show colors differently

    **Solution:**
    1. Accept this as normal variance
    2. Add disclaimer: "Colors may vary by screen"
    3. Focus on visualizing style, not exact color match

    **Cannot be fixed:** Screen color calibration is device-specific.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Product-Specific Image Issues

### Issue: Certain product types don't render well

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Highly Textured Fabrics">
    **Problem:** Cable knits, bouclé, heavy textures

    **What it looks like:**
    - Texture is flattened or simplified
    - Less realistic fabric appearance

    **Solution:**
    1. Accept this as a limitation for very textured items
    2. Test product first before enabling try-on
    3. If result doesn't meet standards, disable try-on for that product
    4. Focus on simpler textures

    **Note:** AI does its best, but very complex textures are challenging.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Sheer or Transparent Fabrics">
    **Problem:** See-through materials, mesh panels

    **What it looks like:**
    - Transparency not accurately rendered
    - Solid appearance instead of sheer

    **Solution:**
    1. Use product images with high contrast
    2. Show sheer elements clearly
    3. Test result quality before enabling
    4. Consider disabling if result is misleading

    **Workaround:** Layer sheer items over solid base in product photo.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Heavy Embellishments">
    **Problem:** Sequins, beading, 3D details

    **What it looks like:**
    - Fine details simplified or missing
    - Flatter appearance than actual product

    **Solution:**
    1. Test these products individually
    2. If result quality is acceptable, enable
    3. If details are lost, consider disabling
    4. Focus on overall style visualization, not exact detail

    **Note:** Overall garment appearance is more important than tiny details.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Improving Product Images

### Quick Fixes You Can Do Now

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Remove Background">
    **Tools:**
    - Remove.bg (free, automatic)
    - Photoshop (manual, precise)
    - Canva (easy, web-based)

    **Steps:**
    1. Upload product image
    2. Remove background automatically
    3. Replace with solid white or gray
    4. Download and re-upload to Shopify
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Adjust Lighting">
    **Tools:**
    - Photoshop, Lightroom
    - Free: GIMP, Photopea
    - Mobile: Snapseed, VSCO

    **Adjustments:**
    - Increase exposure: +20-30%
    - Reduce shadows: +30-40%
    - Increase contrast: +10-20%
    - Adjust white balance: To neutral
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Crop and Center">
    **Tools:**
    - Shopify image editor
    - Canva
    - Any image editor

    **Best practices:**
    - Center garment in frame
    - Include full product (neckline to hem)
    - Remove excess whitespace
    - Maintain aspect ratio
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Increase Resolution">
    **Warning:** Don't artificially upscale

    **If image is too small:**
    - Re-shoot at higher resolution
    - Use higher quality source files
    - Export from design software at higher resolution

    **Don't:**
    - Upscale small images (creates blur)
    - Use AI upscalers (can introduce artifacts)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Image Quality Checklist

Before enabling try-on, verify each product image:

**Background:**
- [ ] Plain white, gray, or solid neutral color
- [ ] No patterns, textures, or busy elements
- [ ] High contrast with garment

**Lighting:**
- [ ] Soft, even illumination
- [ ] No harsh shadows
- [ ] Bright enough to see all details

**Composition:**
- [ ] Garment centered in frame
- [ ] Front-facing, straight-on angle
- [ ] Full product visible (not cropped)
- [ ] 60-80% of frame occupied by garment

**Technical:**
- [ ] Minimum 800px width (1200px+ recommended)
- [ ] High-quality JPEG (90%+) or PNG
- [ ] File size < 5MB
- [ ] Sharp focus, not blurry

<Check>
Products meeting all these criteria will deliver the best virtual try-on experience.
</Check>

## Testing Image Quality

### Process:

1. **Upload test selfie** in good lighting, facing camera
2. **Try on the product** on your live store
3. **Evaluate result:**
   - Does it look realistic?
   - Are colors accurate?
   - Is placement correct?
   - Would this help a customer decide?

4. **If result is good:** Enable for customers
5. **If result is poor:** Improve image and retest

### Example Comparison:

**Poor Image:**
- Busy background (retail store)
- Uneven lighting (shadows on left)
- Low resolution (600px)
- Angled product (not facing forward)

**Result:** Inaccurate rendering, fuzzy edges

**Good Image:**
- Plain white background
- Soft, even lighting
- High resolution (1500px)
- Centered, front-facing product

**Result:** Realistic, accurate rendering

## When to Disable Try-On

If you can't improve image quality enough:

**Consider disabling try-on for products with:**
- Very complex textures that don't render well
- Heavy embellishments that lose detail
- Sheer fabrics that don't translate
- Low-quality source images you can't replace

**Why disable:** It's better to not offer try-on than to offer poor-quality try-on that doesn't help customers.

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use model photos or must I use flat lays?">
    Both work. Model photos often perform better for dresses and structured garments. Flat lays work well for simple tops. Choose based on what you have available.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need professional photography?">
    Not necessarily, but you need good lighting, plain backgrounds, and decent resolution. DIY photos with proper setup can work great.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can Looksy fix poor product images automatically?">
    No. Looksy uses your images as-is. If you provide poor images, results will be poor. "Garbage in, garbage out" applies to AI.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I have thousands of products with varying image quality?">
    Start by enabling try-on on products with the best images. Gradually improve and add more products over time. Don't enable everything at once.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
