## Why does a try-on result look wrong?

A weak preview can come from the selected product or variant, product imagery, the shopper photo, a browser/upload problem, a generation error, or a known limitation of visual try-on. Change one input at a time so you can identify the cause.

## Check the selected item

- Confirm the product and variant match the preview request.
- Review whether the product images clearly show the chosen color, pattern, silhouette, and important details.
- Try another available product image or angle if the merchant controls allow it.
- Test a second product to determine whether the problem is isolated.

## Check the shopper photo

Use a clear photo of one real person with the body area relevant to the product visible. Avoid heavy blur, darkness, obstruction, extreme crops, or multiple people. A full-body photo is not required for every product, but the model needs enough visible context to place the item.

## Check the upload path

The current storefront handles common formats including JPG, PNG, HEIC, and WebP. If selection or upload fails, verify browser permission, retry with a smaller clear image, and compare another current browser or device.

## Set the right expectation

Looksy creates a visual preview. It does not measure the shopper, predict a clothing size, or guarantee fit, drape, texture, or color under every condition. Keep size charts, garment measurements, fabric details, and return-policy information available.

## Escalate with useful evidence

Before contacting [Looksy support](mailto:caleb@withlooksy.com), collect:

- store, product, and variant URL;
- theme and product template;
- device, browser, time, and timezone;
- exact error text or a screenshot of the preview;
- whether another product or photo works;
- the troubleshooting steps already tried.

Do not attach a shopper's private image to ordinary email without consent and an approved support process.
