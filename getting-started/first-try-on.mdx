## Experience Virtual Try-On Yourself

Before promoting virtual try-on to customers, experience it yourself. This helps you understand the customer journey and identify any optimization opportunities.

## Step 1: Navigate to a Product Page

1. Open your Shopify store in a browser
2. Go to a product page where you've enabled virtual try-on
3. Ensure you see the **"Try it on"** button

<Tip>
Test on both desktop and mobile. Mobile accounts for 70%+ of traffic, so the mobile experience is critical.
</Tip>

## Step 2: Click "Try it on"

Click the button to launch the virtual try-on experience.

**What happens:**
- A modal or overlay opens
- You're prompted to upload or take a photo
- Instructions guide you through the process

## Step 3: Upload a Selfie

You have two options:

### Option A: Upload an Existing Photo

1. Click **"Upload Photo"**
2. Select a photo from your device
3. Choose a recent, front-facing selfie

### Option B: Take a New Photo

1. Click **"Take Photo"** (on mobile)
2. Allow camera access when prompted
3. Face the camera directly
4. Take the selfie

<Check>
**Best selfie practices:**
- Good lighting (natural or bright indoor light)
- Face the camera directly
- Neutral expression
- No sunglasses or hats
- Recent photo
</Check>

## Step 4: Wait for the Result

After uploading:
- The AI processes the image (3-5 seconds)
- A loading indicator shows progress
- The virtual try-on result appears

## Step 5: Evaluate the Result

Ask yourself:

- **Does it look realistic?** Is the garment positioned naturally on your body?
- **Are colors accurate?** Does the color match the product image?
- **Is placement correct?** Does the neckline, sleeve, and hem sit right?
- **Would this help you decide?** If you were a customer, would this build purchase confidence?

### If the Result Looks Great

Congratulations! Your virtual try-on is working well. Move on to promoting the feature.

### If the Result Looks Off

Check these factors:

- **Product image quality** – Is the background plain? Is lighting good?
- **Selfie quality** – Was lighting poor? Was the angle awkward?
- **Garment type** – Some products (highly textured, oversized) may not work as well

[Troubleshoot image quality issues →](/troubleshooting/image-quality)

## Step 6: Test on Mobile

Repeat the process on your smartphone:

1. Open your store on your phone's browser
2. Navigate to the same product page
3. Click **"Try it on"**
4. Take a selfie with your phone's camera
5. Evaluate the mobile experience

### Mobile-Specific Checks

- Is the button easy to tap?
- Does the camera open smoothly?
- Is the upload process intuitive?
- Does the result render quickly?
- Does it look good on a small screen?

<Warning>
If the mobile experience is clunky, customers won't use it. Mobile optimization is critical.
</Warning>

## Step 7: Ask Team Members to Test

Get feedback from others:

1. Ask teammates to try virtual try-on on different products
2. Have them use their own phones and selfies
3. Gather feedback on the experience
4. Identify any issues or confusion points

### Questions to Ask

- Was it easy to find the button?
- Was the process intuitive?
- Did the result look accurate?
- Would you use this when shopping?
- Did it work well on your device?

## Step 8: Test Different Products

Try virtual try-on on multiple product types:

- **Simple tops** – See how basic garments render
- **Patterned items** – Check if patterns display accurately
- **Dresses** – Test more complex garments
- **Jackets** – See how outerwear looks

Identify which product categories work best.

## Step 9: Compare Against Product Photos

**Side-by-side comparison:**

Place your phone next to the product image on your desktop. Does the virtual try-on look consistent with the product photo in terms of:
- Color accuracy
- Pattern placement
- Overall style

If there are significant discrepancies, check your product image quality.

## Common First Try-On Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The result doesn't look accurate">
    **Likely causes:**
    - Poor product image quality (busy background, bad lighting)
    - Poor selfie quality (dark, angled, low resolution)
    - Product type not ideal for virtual try-on (very textured, oversized)

    **Solution:** Re-test with better photos. If issues persist, that product may not be suitable for try-on.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The button doesn't appear">
    **Likely causes:**
    - App embed not enabled in theme settings
    - Product not enabled in Looksy dashboard
    - Browser cache needs clearing

    **Solution:** Check theme settings (Online Store → Themes → Customize → App embeds). Ensure product is enabled in Looksy dashboard.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Upload is slow or fails">
    **Likely causes:**
    - Large file size (over 5MB)
    - Slow internet connection
    - Browser compatibility issue

    **Solution:** Use smaller images, test on a different browser, ensure stable internet connection.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Mobile experience is clunky">
    **Likely causes:**
    - Button placement not optimized for mobile
    - Theme CSS conflicts
    - Slow page load times

    **Solution:** Customize button placement in theme editor, test on multiple devices, optimize page speed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What to Do After Your First Try-On

### If Everything Works Well

1. **Enable more products** – Scale to more items in categories that work
2. **Promote the feature** – Add messaging to your homepage and marketing
3. **Track performance** – Monitor engagement and conversions in analytics

### If There Are Issues

1. **Troubleshoot** – Use our [troubleshooting guide](/troubleshooting/common-issues)
2. **Improve image quality** – Re-shoot or edit product photos
3. **Contact support** – We're here to help at [support@looksy.ai](mailto:support@looksy.ai)

## Testing Checklist

Before going live to customers:

- [ ] Test virtual try-on on desktop
- [ ] Test virtual try-on on mobile
- [ ] Try multiple product types
- [ ] Use different selfies (different lighting, angles)
- [ ] Ask team members to test
- [ ] Verify button is visible and easy to find
- [ ] Check that results look accurate and realistic
- [ ] Ensure mobile experience is smooth
- [ ] Confirm fast rendering (3-5 seconds)

<Check>
Once you've completed this checklist and are satisfied with the experience, you're ready to promote virtual try-on to customers!
</Check>
