## Maximizing Virtual Try-On Performance

Following these best practices will help you get the most out of Looksy, increase conversion rates, and reduce returns.

## 1. Start with High-Performing Products

### Focus on Quick Wins

Don't enable virtual try-on for your entire catalog immediately. Instead:

- **Start with bestsellers** – Products that already convert well will show measurable lift
- **Target high-return items** – Products with fit issues benefit most from try-on
- **Choose visually clear products** – Simple, well-photographed items work best initially

<Tip>
Enable 10-20 products first, measure performance for 2 weeks, then expand based on data.
</Tip>

### Expand Strategically

Once you have baseline data:
- **Scale to collections** that perform well
- **Add new releases** to build excitement
- **Enable seasonal items** during peak shopping periods

## 2. Customize Button Styling

### Make the Try-On Button Stand Out

The "Try On" button appears on your product images. You can customize:
- **Button text** – "Try On" (default) or custom copy
- **Colors** – Text and background colors to match your brand
- **Size** – Font size and button padding
- **Position** – Top left or top right corner

### A/B Test Button Copy

Experiment with different call-to-action phrases:
- "Try On" (default)
- "See it on you"
- "Virtual try-on"
- "Preview on yourself"

<Note>
Most stores see highest engagement with "Try On" or "See it on you". Customize in Looksy admin → Customize Button.
</Note>

## 3. Communicate the Benefits

### Add Messaging Around Try-On

Help shoppers understand what they're getting:

**On Product Pages:**
- "See how this looks on you before buying"
- "Upload a photo and preview the fit"
- "Reduce returns with our virtual try-on"

**In Product Descriptions:**
Mention the try-on feature: "Not sure about the fit? Use our virtual try-on to see it on yourself instantly."

### Use Trust Signals

Reassure shoppers about the technology:
- "AI-powered virtual try-on"
- "Secure and private – images are not stored"
- "Works on any device"

## 4. Optimize for Mobile

### Mobile Accounts for 70%+ of Shopify Traffic

Ensure your virtual try-on experience is mobile-first:

- **Fast load times** – Keep page speed optimized
- **Large, tappable button** – Easy to click on small screens
- **Smooth upload flow** – Simple camera access and photo selection
- **Responsive results** – Try-on renders quickly and clearly on mobile

<Check>
Test the entire try-on flow on your own phone before launch. If it feels clunky, customers won't use it.
</Check>

## 5. Leverage Analytics to Refine Strategy

### Track Key Metrics

Monitor these indicators in your Looksy dashboard:

- **Try-on engagement rate** – % of visitors who use try-on
- **Conversion rate lift** – Products with vs. without try-on
- **Time on page** – Do shoppers spend longer on try-on enabled products?
- **Return rate** – Track if returns decrease for try-on products

### Iterate Based on Data

- **Disable underperforming products** – If a product has low engagement, the images may not be suitable
- **Double down on winners** – Expand to similar products in the same category
- **Seasonal adjustments** – Promote try-on during high-traffic periods

## 6. Set Proper Customer Expectations

### Be Transparent About What to Expect

**Do:**
- "See how the style and color look on you"
- "Visualize the fit before you buy"
- "Get a realistic preview of the garment"

**Don't:**
- Overpromise on fit accuracy
- Claim it's a perfect representation of drape and texture
- Suggest it replaces size charts (it complements them)

### Combine with Size Guides

Virtual try-on is most effective when paired with:
- Detailed size charts
- Fit descriptions (e.g., "runs small," "oversized fit")
- Customer reviews mentioning fit

## 7. Maximize Product Image Variety

### Use Multiple Product Images

Looksy uses up to 4 product images per try-on for accuracy:
- **Different angles** – Show front, side, back, detail views
- **Close-ups** – Highlight texture, material, and fabric details
- **Design elements** – Showcase unique features, patterns, embellishments
- **Any resolution works** – Higher resolution helps but isn't required

### Add Images to New Products

As you add new products:
- **Upload multiple angles** (max 4 images used by AI)
- **Include detail shots** showing texture and material
- **Test try-on results** to verify they look realistic
- **Works with any photography style** – No need to reshoot or adjust images

## 8. Promote Virtual Try-On Across Channels

### Drive Awareness

Customers can't use a feature they don't know exists:

**On-Site:**
- Homepage banner: "Try clothes on virtually before you buy"
- Collection pages: "All items available for virtual try-on"
- Cart page: "Unsure? Go back and try it on"

**Email Marketing:**
- Feature try-on in new product launch emails
- Include in abandoned cart emails: "Still unsure? Try On virtually"
- Promote in seasonal campaigns

**Social Media:**
- Share customer try-on examples (with permission)
- Create tutorials showing how to use the feature
- Highlight products that look great with try-on

## 9. Encourage Engagement with Incentives

### Make Try-On Rewarding

- **Lead capture:** Collect emails during try-on with a small discount incentive
- **Exclusive previews:** Let VIP customers try on new releases early
- **Social sharing:** Encourage customers to share try-on results (with privacy controls)

<Tip>
Offer a 5-10% discount for first-time try-on users to drive initial engagement and data collection.
</Tip>

## 10. Continuously Test and Optimize

### Run Regular Experiments

- **Button copy and styling** – Test different text, colors, and sizes
- **Messaging variations** – Try different copy around the try-on feature
- **Product mix** – Rotate which products have try-on enabled
- **Mobile vs. desktop** – Optimize button styling separately for each platform

### Stay Updated

- **Monitor Looksy updates** – New features and improvements roll out regularly
- **Review analytics monthly** – Identify trends and opportunities
- **Gather customer feedback** – Ask shoppers what they think of the feature

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

<Warning>
**Don't:**
- Enable try-on for very revealing products (may fail to generate)
- Use confusing or unclear button text
- Ignore mobile optimization
- Forget to test the feature yourself regularly
- Set unrealistic customer expectations about fit and sizing
</Warning>

## Quick Wins Checklist

Start here for immediate impact:

- [ ] Enable try-on for your top 10 bestselling products
- [ ] Upload multiple product angles and detail shots (max 4 images per product)
- [ ] Customize button styling (colors, text) in Looksy admin
- [ ] Test the flow on mobile and desktop
- [ ] Add messaging to product descriptions
- [ ] Set up conversion tracking in analytics
- [ ] Promote the feature on your homepage
- [ ] Monitor performance weekly and adjust
