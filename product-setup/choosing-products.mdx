## Which Products Work Best with Virtual Try-On?

Looksy works best with **apparel and clothing items** that shoppers typically want to visualize on themselves before purchasing. The technology is optimized for garments that can be clearly photographed and digitally rendered on different body types.

## Product Selection Strategy

### Enable by Collection (Recommended)

The fastest way to get started is to enable virtual try-on for entire collections at once.

**Benefits:**
- All products in the collection are automatically enabled
- New products added to the collection are automatically included
- Easy to manage and scale
- Consistent customer experience across product lines

**Best for:**
- New releases or seasonal collections
- Product categories that consistently work well (e.g., "All Dresses")
- Stores with well-organized collection structures

### Enable by Individual Product

For more control, enable virtual try-on product by product.

**Benefits:**
- Fine-tuned control over which products offer try-on
- Test performance on specific SKUs
- Gradually roll out the feature

**Best for:**
- Testing virtual try-on with bestsellers first
- Stores with mixed inventory (some products work better than others)
- Premium or high-margin products where conversion impact matters most

## Products That Work Best

### Garments with Clear Structure

Virtual try-on delivers the best results for products with:
- **Defined shapes and silhouettes** (not overly drapey or loose)
- **Visible necklines and armholes**
- **Clear patterns and graphics** that can be accurately displayed
- **Solid construction** (structured tops perform better than sheer fabrics)

### Products with High Return Rates

Focus on products where customers struggle with fit visualization:
- Items with unique cuts or styles
- Size-dependent garments
- Products with above-average return rates due to fit issues
- New styles that customers haven't seen on real people yet

## Products That Need Extra Consideration

### Highly Detailed or Textured Items

Products with intricate details may not render perfectly:
- Heavy embellishments (sequins, beading, 3D appliques)
- Complex lace or cutout patterns
- Highly textured fabrics (cable knits, bouclé)

**Tip:** These can still work, but test them first to ensure the virtual try-on meets your quality standards.

### Accessories and Non-Apparel

Currently, Looksy is optimized for apparel worn on the upper body. The following categories have limitations:
- Jewelry and accessories
- Hats and headwear
- Pants and bottoms (coming soon)
- Shoes and footwear

## Testing Your Product Selection

After enabling products:

1. **Visit the product page** on your live store
2. **Click "Try it on"** and upload a test selfie
3. **Evaluate the result** – Does it look realistic? Would it help a shopper make a decision?
4. **Adjust as needed** – If certain products don't meet your standards, disable them individually

<Tip>
Start with your 10-20 bestselling products in categories that work well (tops, dresses, jackets). This gives you quick wins and valuable data before expanding to more products.
</Tip>

## Performance Indicators

Track these metrics to identify which products benefit most from virtual try-on:

- **Engagement rate** – What percentage of visitors click "Try it on"?
- **Conversion lift** – Do products with try-on convert better?
- **Return rate** – Are returns lower for products with virtual try-on?
- **Time on page** – Do shoppers spend more time on products with try-on enabled?

Use this data to refine your product selection strategy over time.
