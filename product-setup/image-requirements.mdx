## Why Image Quality Matters

The quality of your product images directly impacts how realistic and accurate the virtual try-on appears. Clear, well-lit photos on plain backgrounds deliver the best results.

## Required Image Specifications

### Background

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Plain, Solid Backgrounds (Highly Recommended)">
    **Best choice:** White, light gray, or solid neutral backgrounds

    **Why it works:**
    - AI can cleanly isolate the garment
    - No distracting elements
    - Fastest processing
    - Most accurate try-on results

    **Examples:** White backdrop, light gray studio background, beige seamless paper
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Simple Backgrounds (Acceptable)">
    **Works but not ideal:** Minimal background with clear product separation

    **Requirements:**
    - High contrast between product and background
    - No busy patterns or textures
    - Avoid shadows that blend with the garment

    **Examples:** Simple indoor settings, minimal home backgrounds
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Complex Backgrounds (Not Recommended)">
    **Avoid:** Busy patterns, outdoor scenes, cluttered settings

    **Issues:**
    - AI may struggle to isolate the garment
    - Inaccurate rendering
    - Lower quality try-on experience

    **Examples:** Outdoor locations, patterned walls, busy retail environments
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Lighting

**Professional, even lighting is critical:**

- **Soft, diffused light** – Avoid harsh shadows
- **Front-lit subjects** – Light should illuminate the garment evenly
- **Consistent brightness** – No overexposed or dark areas
- **Natural or studio lighting** – Avoid colored or mood lighting

<Warning>
Harsh shadows, backlighting, or uneven exposure will reduce try-on accuracy.
</Warning>

### Product Angle and Framing

**Front-facing, centered garments work best:**

- **Straight-on view** – Product facing the camera directly
- **Fully visible** – Entire garment should be in frame
- **Centered composition** – Product in the middle of the frame
- **Minimal cropping** – Include full neckline, sleeves, and hemline when possible

**Camera angle:**
- **Eye-level perspective** – Not shot from above or below
- **Flat or model photography** – Both work well if properly lit and framed

### Image Resolution

- **Minimum:** 800px wide
- **Recommended:** 1200px+ wide for crisp, detailed rendering
- **File format:** JPG or PNG
- **File size:** Under 5MB for optimal page speed

## Flat Lay vs. Model Photography

### Flat Lay Photography

**Pros:**
- Consistent angles and lighting
- Budget-friendly
- Easy to replicate across products

**Cons:**
- May not show garment drape realistically
- Harder to visualize fit

**Best for:** T-shirts, simple tops, graphic apparel

### Model Photography

**Pros:**
- Shows fit and drape more realistically
- Easier for AI to map garment placement
- Better for dresses and structured garments

**Cons:**
- More expensive to produce
- Need consistent model poses

**Best for:** Dresses, jackets, fitted tops, structured apparel

<Tip>
If using model photography, ensure models are facing forward with arms down or slightly away from the body. Avoid overly stylized poses.
</Tip>

## Common Image Problems and Solutions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Problem: Dark or Shadowy Images">
    **Solution:** Re-shoot with better lighting or use photo editing to increase brightness and reduce shadows. Aim for soft, even illumination.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Problem: Busy or Patterned Backgrounds">
    **Solution:** Use background removal tools to isolate the garment, or re-shoot on a plain backdrop. Looksy works best with clean backgrounds.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Problem: Garment Not Centered or Cropped">
    **Solution:** Ensure the product fills at least 60% of the frame and is centered. Include full necklines and sleeves when possible.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Problem: Low Resolution or Pixelated">
    **Solution:** Use higher resolution source images. Minimum 800px width, ideally 1200px+ for best results.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Problem: Garment Blends with Background">
    **Solution:** Increase contrast between garment and background. Use a white backdrop for dark clothes, or a gray backdrop for light clothes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Optimizing Existing Product Photos

If you can't re-shoot products, here are quick improvements:

1. **Remove backgrounds** – Use tools like Remove.bg or Photoshop to isolate garments
2. **Adjust brightness** – Increase exposure for dark images
3. **Crop strategically** – Center the garment and remove unnecessary space
4. **Test and iterate** – Upload test photos and see which perform best

## Image Quality Checklist

Before enabling virtual try-on, verify each product image:

- [ ] Plain or simple background (white/gray preferred)
- [ ] Even, soft lighting with no harsh shadows
- [ ] Front-facing, centered garment
- [ ] Full product visible (neckline, sleeves, hem)
- [ ] High resolution (1200px+ width)
- [ ] Good contrast between product and background
- [ ] No busy patterns or distracting elements

<Check>
Products with images that meet these criteria will deliver the best virtual try-on experience and highest conversion rates.
</Check>
