## Overview

Looksy supports **full body apparel try-on** and delivers excellent results for all types of clothing items that shoppers want to visualize on themselves before purchasing.

## Fully Supported Products

These product types work excellently with Looksy virtual try-on:

### Tops & Shirts ✓

- T-shirts and graphic tees
- Tank tops and camisoles
- Blouses and button-up shirts
- Polo shirts
- Casual and dress shirts
- Crop tops
- Long-sleeve tops

**Why they work:** Clear silhouettes, defined necklines, and simple structures render accurately.

### Dresses ✓

- Casual dresses
- Cocktail and formal dresses
- Maxi dresses
- Midi dresses
- Mini dresses
- Shirt dresses
- Bodycon dresses
- A-line dresses

**Why they work:** Dresses have high customer uncertainty, making virtual try-on highly valuable.

### Outerwear ✓

- Jackets (denim, leather, bomber)
- Blazers and suit jackets
- Coats and parkas
- Hoodies (pullover and zip-up)
- Cardigans
- Vests
- Windbreakers

**Why they work:** Structured garments with clear shapes render well.

### Activewear ✓

- Sports tops and athletic shirts
- Yoga tops and pants
- Running shirts and shorts
- Compression tops and leggings
- Sports bras (when appropriately photographed)
- Gym hoodies
- Athletic jackets
- Athletic pants and joggers

**Why they work:** Fitted activewear benefits from full-body visualization, reducing fit uncertainty for complete outfits.

### Swimwear ✓

- One-piece swimsuits
- Bikini tops
- Tankinis
- Rash guards
- Swim tops

**Why they work:** High purchase hesitation makes virtual try-on valuable for conversions.

### Pants and Bottoms ✓

- Jeans and trousers
- Shorts
- Skirts
- Leggings
- Joggers
- Athletic pants
- Dress pants

**Why they work:** Full body rendering allows customers to see complete outfits and visualize how bottoms fit their body type.

## Products with Limitations

### Highly Detailed or Embellished Items

**May require testing:**
- Heavy sequins or beading
- Complex lace patterns
- 3D appliques
- Intricate cutouts

**Why:** Very fine details may be simplified during rendering. Test these products individually to ensure quality meets your standards.

### Sheer or Transparent Fabrics

**May require testing:**
- Sheer blouses
- Mesh panels
- See-through materials
- Very lightweight fabrics

**Why:** Transparency can be challenging to render accurately. Works best with high-contrast images.

### Oversized or Drapey Styles

**May require testing:**
- Very oversized hoodies
- Flowing, drapey cardigans
- Loose, unstructured garments

**Why:** Lack of structure can make garment placement less precise. Often still works, but test first.

## Not Currently Supported

### Accessories

**Not supported:**
- Jewelry (necklaces, bracelets, earrings)
- Hats and headwear
- Scarves and wraps
- Bags and purses
- Belts

**Why:** These require different rendering technology focused on accessories rather than apparel.

### Footwear

**Not supported:**
- Shoes and sneakers
- Boots
- Sandals
- Slippers

**Why:** Footwear requires foot and leg rendering, outside the current upper-body focus.

### Non-Apparel

**Not supported:**
- Home goods
- Furniture
- Electronics
- Beauty products

**Why:** Looksy is purpose-built for apparel try-on.

## Product Combinations

### Single Item Try-On

Customers can currently try on **one item at a time** for optimal accuracy.

**Supported:**
- One top
- One dress
- One jacket

**Not currently supported:**
- Multiple items simultaneously (e.g., top + jacket)
- Outfit combinations

<Tip>
Single-item focus ensures the highest accuracy and rendering quality.
</Tip>

## Image Requirements by Product Type

Different products may have specific image needs:

### Tops & Shirts

- **Best angle:** Front-facing, flat lay or model
- **Background:** Plain white or gray
- **Lighting:** Even, soft illumination
- **Framing:** Full garment visible

### Dresses

- **Best angle:** Front-facing, model photography preferred
- **Background:** Plain, solid color
- **Lighting:** Shows fabric drape clearly
- **Framing:** Full dress visible, including hem

### Outerwear

- **Best angle:** Front-facing, can be worn or flat
- **Background:** Plain, neutral
- **Lighting:** Shows texture and structure
- **Framing:** Full jacket/coat, including sleeves

## Testing Product Compatibility

Before enabling virtual try-on at scale:

1. **Select 3-5 representative products** from each category
2. **Enable virtual try-on** for those test products
3. **Test with real selfies** – Upload your own or ask team members
4. **Evaluate quality** – Does the result meet your standards?
5. **Expand or adjust** – If successful, enable more in that category

## Product Selection Strategy

### Start with High-Confidence Categories

Focus initially on:
- Simple tops and t-shirts
- Structured dresses
- Jackets and blazers

These categories consistently deliver excellent results.

### Expand Strategically

Once you validate performance:
- Add more complex garments
- Test new styles
- Scale to full catalog

### When to Skip Virtual Try-On

Consider not enabling try-on for:
- Very low-quality product images
- Products with extreme embellishments
- Items that don't photograph well
- Non-apparel products

<Check>
It's better to have virtual try-on on fewer products with great results than on many products with mediocre results.
</Check>

## Common Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can customers try on multiple items at once?">
    Not currently. Looksy focuses on single-item try-on for maximum accuracy. Multi-item try-on is on our roadmap.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will lower body products be supported in the future?">
    Yes. Lower body rendering (pants, shorts, skirts) is in active development. We prioritize delivering excellent accuracy before launching new product types.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use virtual try-on for custom or made-to-order products?">
    Yes, as long as the product type is supported and you have high-quality product images. Virtual try-on works with any apparel, including custom and made-to-order items.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if my product has very intricate details?">
    Test it first. Upload a selfie and see if the result meets your quality standards. If fine details don't render well, you can choose not to enable try-on for that specific product.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
