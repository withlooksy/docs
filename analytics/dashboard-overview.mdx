## What does the Looksy analytics dashboard show?

The Looksy analytics area reports observed activity connected to the try-on experience. Use it to understand adoption and assisted shopping behavior, but do not treat an association as proof that Looksy caused a sale or prevented a return.

Open **Apps → Looksy → Analytics** in Shopify admin. The metrics available to you can depend on your date range, connected integrations, and tracking access.

## Core metrics

The current analytics experience may include:

- **Sessions:** buyer sessions in which Looksy engagement was observed.
- **Try-ons:** try-on events recorded during the selected period.
- **Add to cart:** Looksy-engaged sessions that included an add-to-cart event.
- **Conversion rate:** the share of observed Looksy sessions that also included add to cart; this is not necessarily the store's completed-purchase conversion rate.
- **Assisted cart value:** value added to cart after a try-on; it is not completed revenue.
- **Try-on revenue:** completed purchase value attributed under the definition shown in the dashboard, when checkout tracking is available.
- **Emails captured:** consented captures from the try-on prompt when the required email integration is connected.

Read the caption and tooltip beside a metric before comparing it. An unavailable or partial metric should remain unavailable rather than being estimated from another number.

## How to use the dashboard

1. Choose a date range with enough activity to reduce day-to-day noise.
2. Record try-ons and Looksy-engaged sessions as the adoption baseline.
3. Review product-level activity to find where shoppers use try-on.
4. Compare equivalent periods and note promotions, theme changes, inventory changes, or tracking gaps.
5. Use Shopify's order and return data for completed commercial outcomes.
6. Test one change at a time before assigning a result to Looksy.

<Note>
Analytics is observational. Phrase conclusions as "associated with" unless a controlled experiment supports a causal claim.
</Note>

## Data and privacy checks

Before relying on a report, verify the selected dates, integration status, event coverage, consent flow, and currency. Review the current [Looksy privacy policy](https://withlooksy.com/legal/privacy-policy) and your store's own obligations for analytics and customer data.
