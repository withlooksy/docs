## Understanding Your Looksy Dashboard

The Looksy analytics dashboard provides real-time insights into how virtual try-on drives engagement and conversions on your store.

## Accessing Your Dashboard

1. Go to **Apps** in your Shopify admin
2. Click **Looksy**
3. Navigate to **Analytics** tab

Or access directly from the Looksy app embed in your Shopify admin.

## Engagement Metrics

### Try-On Usage

**What it shows:**
- Total try-ons over time
- Daily/weekly try-on volume
- Peak usage times

**Why it matters:**
- Indicates feature adoption
- Shows which products generate most interest
- Helps plan inventory and marketing

**What to aim for:**
- 30-50% of product page visitors use try-on
- Steady growth week-over-week
- Higher engagement on featured products

### Engagement Rate by Product

**What it shows:**
- Which products have highest try-on usage
- % of visitors who try on each product
- Comparison across categories

**Why it matters:**
- Identifies best-performing products
- Reveals which categories benefit most
- Guides product enablement strategy

**How to use it:**
1. Sort by engagement rate (highest first)
2. Identify top performers
3. Enable try-on on similar products
4. Disable try-on on low-engagement products

<Check>
Products with 40%+ engagement rates are ideal candidates for virtual try-on.
</Check>

### Engagement Rate by Device

**What it shows:**
- Mobile vs. desktop try-on usage
- Device-specific engagement patterns

**Why it matters:**
- Mobile accounts for 70%+ of traffic
- Identifies optimization opportunities
- Guides mobile vs. desktop strategy

**Typical breakdown:**
- Mobile: 60-75% of try-ons
- Desktop: 25-40% of try-ons

## Conversion Metrics

### Conversion Rate Lift

**What it shows:**
- Conversion rate for products with try-on
- Conversion rate for products without try-on
- % lift attributed to virtual try-on

**Why it matters:**
- **Direct ROI indicator**
- Proves business value
- Justifies investment

**What to aim for:**
- 10-25% conversion rate lift
- Consistent lift across product categories
- Growing lift over time as awareness increases

### Add-to-Cart Rate

**What it shows:**
- % of try-on users who add product to cart
- Comparison to non-try-on users

**Why it matters:**
- Measures immediate purchase intent
- Indicates try-on effectiveness
- Helps identify friction points

**Typical performance:**
- 40-60% of try-on users add to cart
- 2x higher than non-try-on users

### Revenue Attribution

**What it shows:**
- Total revenue from customers who used try-on
- Average order value (try-on vs. non-try-on)
- Revenue lift percentage

**Why it matters:**
- **Bottom-line impact**
- Calculates ROI
- Demonstrates business value

**How it's calculated:**
```
Revenue Impact = (Orders with Try-On × AOV) -
                 (Expected Orders without Try-On × AOV)
```

<Note>
Revenue attribution uses last-touch attribution: If a customer uses try-on and purchases within the session, it's attributed to virtual try-on.
</Note>

## Product Performance

### Top Performing Products

**Table shows:**
- Product name and image
- Total try-ons
- Engagement rate
- Conversion rate
- Revenue generated

**Sort by:**
- Most try-ons
- Highest engagement rate
- Highest conversion rate
- Most revenue

**Use cases:**
1. **Identify winners:** Which products benefit most?
2. **Expand strategy:** Enable try-on on similar products
3. **Marketing focus:** Promote high-performing products

### Low Performing Products

**Filters:**
- Products with < 20% engagement rate
- Products with low conversion lift
- Products with few try-ons

**Actions:**
1. **Improve images:** Poor results often due to image quality
2. **Test different products:** Some categories work better
3. **Disable try-on:** If results don't justify feature

<Warning>
Don't force try-on on products that don't perform well. Focus on high-impact products.
</Warning>

## Customer Behavior Insights

### Try-On Funnel

**Shows customer journey:**
1. **Product page visit** → 100%
2. **Click "Try On"** → 35% (engagement rate)
3. **Upload photo** → 90% (of those who clicked)
4. **View result** → 95% (of those who uploaded)
5. **Add to cart** → 50% (of those who viewed result)
6. **Complete purchase** → 40% (of those who added to cart)

**Drop-off points:**
- Most common: Click → Upload (camera/permission issues)
- Second: Add to cart → Purchase (normal e-commerce friction)

**Optimization opportunities:**
- Improve button visibility (increase click rate)
- Streamline upload flow (reduce upload drop-off)
- Optimize checkout (increase purchase rate)

### Time to Purchase

**What it shows:**
- How long between try-on and purchase
- Immediate vs. delayed purchases

**Typical pattern:**
- 60-70% purchase within same session
- 20-30% return within 24 hours
- 10% purchase later

**Why it matters:**
- Indicates decision-making speed
- Shows confidence built by try-on
- Helps plan retargeting campaigns

## Additional Insights

Track additional metrics in your Shopify analytics to measure the full impact of virtual try-on:

- **Customer satisfaction** – Monitor reviews and feedback mentioning try-on
- **Repeat purchase rate** – Customers who use try-on may return more often
- **Average order value** – Try-on may encourage multi-item purchases

<Note>
Return rate data is not currently tracked in the Looksy dashboard. Use your Shopify analytics to monitor returns if needed.
</Note>

## Understanding Anomalies

### When Metrics Look Unusual

**Possible causes:**
- Marketing campaign (traffic spike)
- Seasonal changes (holidays, sales)
- Website issues (downtime, bugs)
- New product launches

**How to investigate:**
1. Check date range (is it comparing fairly?)
2. Review site changes (new products, campaigns)
3. Look at Shopify analytics (overall traffic changes?)
4. Check for errors (technical issues?)

<Note>
Context matters. Don't panic if metrics fluctuate – look for patterns over weeks and months, not days.
</Note>
